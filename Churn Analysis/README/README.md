## 🎯 Objective
- Analyze customer churn patterns  
- Identify high-risk customers  
- Understand revenue impact due to churn  
- Support data-driven decisions  

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)  
- Data Analysis (Cleaning & Transformation)  
- DAX (Measures & KPIs)  
- Excel/CSV
  ## 📊 Key Insights

- **High Revenue Leakage:**  
  The churn rate stands at **26.54%**, leading to a total revenue loss of **$2.86M**, which is approximately **18% of the total revenue potential**.

- **Contract Vulnerability:**  
  Customers on **month-to-month contracts** exhibit the highest churn risk. In contrast, **two-year contracts** are the most stable and generate the highest revenue (**$6.3M**).

- **Service Correlation:**  
  Lack of **Tech Support** is strongly associated with higher churn. Customers with active support services are significantly more likely to stay.

- **Tenure Critical Zone:**  
  A majority of churn occurs within the **first 5 months**, indicating an early-stage drop-off. This highlights the need for improved **customer onboarding strategies**.

- **Payment Method Friction:**  
  Customers using **Electronic Checks** have a significantly higher churn rate compared to those using **automated payment methods** (Credit Card / Bank Transfer).

- **Gender Neutrality:**  
  Churn distribution is nearly identical between **Male (49.28%)** and **Female (50.72%)**, suggesting that **service-based targeting** is more impactful than demographic segmentation.
